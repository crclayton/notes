Inflationary Gap

When 23#23, there is pressure for factor prices to rise because when producing beyond capacity, inputs are in strong demand. This includes labour, so there will be labour shortages and so wages will rise.

In response to having to pay more for inputs, firms will increase the prices of their products. So the 15#15 curve will shift up, so real GDP will decrease back to 71#71.

Additionally, the increase 15#15 temporarily increases the price level 4#4, which is why it's called inflationary.
