Demand Shocks

When the AD curve shifts right and it opens up an inflationary gap, this is said to be demand inflation. There are two possible outcomes of this scenario, depending on if the central bank chooses to validate or not.

  1. No monetary validation - Excess labour demand increases wages, which increases factor inputs, which increases AS, and the gap is closed at a higher price level

  2. Monetary validation - The bank could increase the money supply to sustain the inflationary gap in an attempt to keep operating at a higher GDP. This changes temporary inflation into sustained inflation.
