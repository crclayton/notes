Costs of Economic Growth

  1. Forgone consumption - Increases in real GDP per capita is often the result of investment expenditure. However, this investment means current consumption is being sacrificed by the current generation consumers. Economic growth promises more goods and services in the future, at the expense of lower consumption now.

  2. Social costs - Economic growth means old firms, capital, and labourers are overtaken by new firms, capital, and labourers. High economic growth means that within a generation workers can find their skills rendered obsolete, which has high social costs.
