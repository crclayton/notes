Automatic Stabilizers

All of the policies discussed above are called discretionary policies because they require an overt decision by the government. However, automatic stabilizers can dampen the effects of shocks without active decisions.

The most common automatic stabilizer is the tax-and-transfer system. Consider a positive AD shock, which would increase real GDP and reduce 4#4. As GDP increases, tax revenues increase and the government makes less transfer payments, so 13#13 increases. This automatically pushes GDP back down, or at least reduces the effects of the shock.
