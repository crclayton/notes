The Process of Disinflation

Disinflation is a reduction in the rate of accelerating, as in when inflation slows down - not to be confused with deflation. Slowing down the rate of inflation - the process of disinflation - occurs in three steps.

  1. Removing monetary validation - The interest rates are increased to lower the money supply and shift the AD curve left. This begins to close the inflationary gap, but is not the end of the story.

  2. Stagflation - Since there still exists an inflationary gap, and since there are expectations of continued inflation, wages continue to rise. So the AS curve still continues to shift upward and the gap continues to close.

    Here there is stagnation because output is falling, and inflation because prices are still rising, hence stagflation.

    The length of time this process continues depends on how backward-looking people are and how long they expect inflation to continue.

  3. Recovery - Finally, output returns to potential.
