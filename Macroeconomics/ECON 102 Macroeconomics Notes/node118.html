Money Demand, 17#17

People have to choose what ratio of money to bonds they want to hold at any given time. If you hold more money, you hold fewer bonds, and vice-versa. The amount of money that everyone collectively holds at any given time is called the demand for money. The willingness to hold money over bonds is called the liquidity preference function, 17#17.
