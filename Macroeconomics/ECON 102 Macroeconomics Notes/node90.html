Supply-side Economics (Reaganomics)

In the 1980s, Reagan and Thatcher tried another method entirely. The idea of supply-side economics was that extensive reductions in tax rates would increase economic activity and increase 21#21 so much, that the amount of tax revenue lost by reducing taxes would be offset by the increase in revenue from the new economic activity.

Instead of returning 2#2 to 21#21 in an inflationary gap, they aimed to close the gap by increasing 21#21. This means inflationary gaps can be closed without moving 14#14. It definitely worked to increase economic activity, but it's a stretch to say it actually increased government tax revenue.
