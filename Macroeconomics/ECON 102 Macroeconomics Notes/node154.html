Exchange Rate

The exchange rate is the amount of domestic currency required to buy one unit of foreign currency. For instance, right now it costs 1.66 CAD to buy 1 GBP, so 123#123. The external value is the inverse, the amount of foreign currency you can buy with one unit of domestic currency, in falling with our previous example, one CAD will buy you 0.60 GBP, so 124#124.

If the CAD appreciates, then the CAD has become more valuable, as in it takes fewer CAD to purchase one GDP, so the exchange rate falls and the external value rises. Similarly, when the CAD depreciates, the exchange rate rises.
