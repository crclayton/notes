Adjustment Process (Short-Run to Long-Run)

The adjustment process evolves the short-run equilibrium to the long-run equilibrium. Let's talk about the key assumptions in each stage.

  1. Short Run

    Factor prices are assumed to be exogenous. So they can change, but any changes are external to the model. Technology and factor supplies are also assumed to be constant, and so potential output 21#21 is constant.

  2. Adjustment of Factor Prices

    In the adjustment process, which we will explore in more detail momentarily. The Factor prices are assumed to adjust in response to output gaps. Technology and factor supplies are similarly assumed to be constant, so 21#21 is constant.

    Essentially, we will examine how the effects of shocks and policies different between the short and long runs. 14#14 and 15#15 shocks have no long-run effect on GDP, and in the adjustment process 2#2 always returns to 21#21.

  3. Long Run

    In the long run, factor prices are fully adjustable in response to output gaps, so 2#2 always returns to 21#21. Technology and factor supplies are also assumed to change, such that 21#21 can grow.

In summary, here is a table of there three stages.
