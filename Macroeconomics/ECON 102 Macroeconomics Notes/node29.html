Aggregate Expenditure

We will distinguish between desired and actual expenditure with the subscript 35#35 for actual. Desired expenditure refers to how much people would like to spend given the resources they actually have, not how much they would spend if they won the lottery. Actual expenditure is predictably what they actually spend.

Aggregate expenditure, 11#11, is the sum of all actual spending in an economy, as in equation (5), and 11#11 is a function of national income, 2#2.
