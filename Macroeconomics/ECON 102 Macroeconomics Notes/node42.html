Simple Multiplier

A change in AE increases 12#12 by more than the initial change in AE. Your national product increases by more than your aggregate expenditure, so you get what you put in plus a little bit extra. This multiple is called the simple multiple, 51#51.

Figure 6: Simple Multiplier

Let's say a Canadian company spends $10 million to construct a factory. This would increase national income by $10 million, but on top of that, the increase in national income would cause an increase in desired consumption because suddenly the carpenters and factories workers have a little bit extra money. So they each buy a TV for their bedroom, so then the TV producers increase production to meet this demand, and so on.

For the increased expenditure, there is larger amount of national income generated. This is dependent on the marginal propensity to spend, 52#52, where the higher the 52#52, the more significant the multiplier.
