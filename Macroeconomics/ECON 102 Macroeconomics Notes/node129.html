Price Changes

Upward shifts in the AS curve will cause prices to rise, so the forces pushing wages up will be inflationary. Downward shifts in the AS curve will cause prices to drop, so the forces pushing wages down will be deflationary.

Inflation caused by wage increases is the sum of three components: output-gap inflation, expected inflation, and supply-shock inflation.
