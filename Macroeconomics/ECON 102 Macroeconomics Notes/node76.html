Contractionary AD Shock

Suppose we start at 71#71. Then there is a negative AD shock. This reduces GDP and reduces the price level, 4#4. After this happens, 73#73, so there is an recessionary gap.

Figure 15: Adjustment Process to Negative AD Shock

In response to the recessionary gap, through the adjustment process discussed above, 2#2 increases back to 21#21, but 4#4 decreases even further. So a contractionary 14#14 shock just decreases 4#4.

Recall that due to sticky wages, these processes are not completely symmetrical. An inflationary gap disappears faster than a recessionary gap, so a negative AD shock has a longer effect.
