Backed Paper Money

Goldsmiths had very secure safes. So people began leaving their gold with them, and in exchange the goldsmith would give them a receipt, stating the goldsmith would return their gold on demand.

Eventually, people began to trade the receipts instead gold, this was basically the first paper money. Then commercial banks began to do this with all sorts of proprietary "bank notes" instead of the goldsmiths. This is what's known as backed money, money that is backed up by gold and is convertible to gold upon demand.
