Money Supply, 16#16

There are multiple definitions of the money supply, but mainly you can consider it as the sum of currency in circulation and bank deposits at chartered banks that can be easily transferable, demand deposits (think checking account).


87#87 (28)

There is also 88#88 which include notice deposits (think savings account), 89#89 which includes deposits at other financial institutions, and 90#90 which includes savings bonds and all other mutual funds. The M1 definition is based emphasizing the medium of exchange, the latter merely include anything considered a store of wealth.
