Non-Factor Payments

When a consumer spends $10, less than $10 is generated as income for the factors of production. This is because of two non-factor payments.

  1. Indirect business taxes - These are consumption taxes collected by business such as GST and PST, not income taxes.

  2. Subsidies - These are similar to consumption taxes but they are the opposite, they are negative taxes, so they must be subtracted.

  3. Depreciation - This is the loss in value of capital via wear and tear. We include this in GDP, but exclude it in Gross National Product.
