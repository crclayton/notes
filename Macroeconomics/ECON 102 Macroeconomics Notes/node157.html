Determination of Exchange Rates

We will consider two cases:

  1. flexible exchange rates - when the central bank makes no transactions in the foreign exchange market.

    Like all other markets, the price for a currency (the exchange rate) is determined by the equilibrium point of the supply and demand curves.

  2. fixed exchange rates - when the central bank chooses to fix the exchange rate at a particular value and makes transactions to offset any excess demand/supply at that value.
